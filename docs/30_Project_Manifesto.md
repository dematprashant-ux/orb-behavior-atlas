# Project Manifesto

**Project:** ORB Behavior Atlas  
**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Living Document  
**Owner:** Prashant Gawade  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-18

---

# 1. Purpose

The ORB Behavior Atlas exists to understand financial markets through disciplined observation, statistical evidence, and reproducible research.

Its purpose is not merely to build trading strategies, but to build a continuously expanding body of knowledge about how markets behave around the Opening Range Breakout (ORB).

Knowledge is the primary product.

Trading strategies are one application of that knowledge.

---

# 2. Mission

To create a research-first quantitative platform that transforms raw market data into validated knowledge, validated knowledge into robust trading strategies, and robust strategies into disciplined portfolio execution.

Every decision should be supported by measurable evidence.

---

# 3. Vision

To become a long-term scientific framework for studying market behavior rather than a collection of isolated trading systems.

The platform should continue evolving through research, validation, and learning while remaining reproducible, transparent, and maintainable.

---

# 4. Core Beliefs

We believe:

- Markets exhibit recurring behaviors.
- Not every observed pattern represents a genuine edge.
- Evidence is stronger than intuition.
- Validation is stronger than optimization.
- Reproducibility is essential.
- Simplicity is preferable to unnecessary complexity.
- Documentation is part of the product.

---

# 5. Research Philosophy

Research begins with observation.

Every hypothesis should be:

- Clearly defined
- Testable
- Measurable
- Reproducible
- Statistically evaluated

Research should seek understanding before profitability.

---

# 6. Engineering Philosophy

Engineering exists to support reliable research.

Software should be:

- Modular
- Deterministic
- Observable
- Maintainable
- Well tested
- Fully documented

Engineering quality protects research quality.

---

# 7. Scientific Principles

The platform is guided by:

- Evidence over assumptions
- Measurement over opinion
- Validation over optimism
- Repeatability over isolated success
- Transparency over hidden logic

Scientific discipline should guide every stage of development.

---

# 8. Development Principles

Development should always prioritize:

- Research before implementation
- Architecture before coding
- Testing before release
- Documentation before deployment
- Risk management before optimization

The process is as important as the outcome.

---

# 9. Long-Term Commitment

The ORB Behavior Atlas is intended to evolve over many years.

The project should continuously:

- Expand its knowledge base
- Improve research quality
- Refine validation methods
- Strengthen engineering practices
- Enhance operational reliability

Progress should be incremental and evidence-based.

---

# 10. What We Will Never Compromise

The project will never compromise on:

- Scientific integrity
- Reproducibility
- Transparency
- Documentation
- Testing
- Risk discipline
- Auditability

These principles take precedence over speed or convenience.

---

# 11. Guiding Statements

The following statements guide every decision:

- Observe before concluding.
- Measure before believing.
- Validate before deploying.
- Document before forgetting.
- Test before trusting.
- Improve continuously.
- Keep the system understandable.
- Preserve the history of every important decision.

---

# 12. Legacy

The value of the ORB Behavior Atlas should not be measured solely by financial performance.

Its lasting contribution is the creation of a structured, evidence-based framework for understanding market behavior that can support future research, education, and quantitative system development.

---

# 13. Closing Manifesto

The ORB Behavior Atlas is built on the belief that disciplined research produces durable knowledge.

Knowledge produces understanding.

Understanding produces better decisions.

Better decisions produce resilient systems.

The project is therefore committed to continuous learning, scientific rigor, engineering excellence, and responsible execution.

Every document, experiment, line of code, validation, and release contributes to a single objective:

**Build a platform where evidence guides every decision, and where knowledge compounds over time.**
