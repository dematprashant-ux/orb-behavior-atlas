# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the identity, mission, scope, and long-term direction of the ORB Behavior Atlas project.

This document serves as the highest-level business and product reference for the repository.

Every architectural, engineering, research, and implementation decision should align with the principles defined here.

---

# 2. Mission

Build a professional, modular, research-driven quantitative trading platform that enables the study, analysis, validation, and eventual automation of intraday market behavior.

The platform should support continuous research while remaining scalable, maintainable, and production-ready.

---

# 3. Vision

Create a platform that combines:

- Quantitative Research
- Market Intelligence
- Historical Behaviour Analysis
- Probability Modeling
- Strategy Development
- Paper Trading
- Live Trading
- Artificial Intelligence Assistance

into one unified ecosystem.

---

# 4. Primary Objectives

The platform shall:

- Collect and organize market data.
- Study market behaviour.
- Build reusable research datasets.
- Discover statistically meaningful patterns.
- Validate trading hypotheses.
- Generate explainable probabilities.
- Support manual and automated trading.
- Preserve research knowledge.
- Continuously improve through data.

---

# 5. Guiding Principles

The project follows these principles:

- Research before implementation.
- Repository is the single source of truth.
- Documentation is part of the product.
- Long-term maintainability over short-term speed.
- Production quality over quick prototypes.
- Decisions must be explainable.
- Simplicity is preferred over unnecessary complexity.
- Modular architecture.
- Continuous improvement.
- Knowledge preservation.

---

# 6. Success Criteria

The project is considered successful when it provides:

- Reliable market data collection.
- Repeatable research workflows.
- Explainable probability models.
- High-quality documentation.
- Stable software architecture.
- Automated trading capabilities.
- Sustainable long-term maintenance.

---

# 7. Scope

The project includes:

- Market Data
- News Intelligence
- Economic Events
- Behaviour Analysis
- Probability Engine
- Strategy Engine
- Research Tools
- Backtesting
- Paper Trading
- Live Trading
- Dashboard
- AI Assistant
- Reporting
- Analytics

---

# 8. Out of Scope

The project is not intended to become:

- A generic brokerage platform.
- A social trading platform.
- A portfolio management application.
- A high-frequency trading system.
- A cryptocurrency exchange.
- A financial advisory service.

Future expansion is possible but is outside the current scope.

---

# 9. Core Values

Every contribution should improve at least one of the following:

- Quality
- Reliability
- Simplicity
- Scalability
- Explainability
- Maintainability
- Reproducibility

---

# 10. Decision Philosophy

When multiple solutions exist, preference should be given to the one that:

1. Solves the actual problem.
2. Fits the existing architecture.
3. Is easier to understand.
4. Is easier to maintain.
5. Is easier to test.
6. Scales for future development.
